Fundo ProClima Moçambique

Para um Futuro Sustentável

I. Context

Window 2 “Private Sector Adaptation”

Mozambique is among the world’s most climate-

vulnerable countries. The National Adaptation Plan

(NAP, 2023) provides for decentralised adaptation

through Local Adaptation Plans (LAPs) at

district/municipality level. However, insufficient

financing and financial transfer mechanisms for

local action present a key barrier.

II. Objective

The Fundo ProClima establishes an innovative and

effective financing mechanism to implement

LAPs.

The ProClima Fund will be implemented by the

Fundação para o Desenvolvimento da Comunidade

(FDC), supported by the Deutsche Gesellschaft für

Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) on behalf of

the German Federal Ministry for Economic

Development (BMZ).

III. Concept

The Fundo ProClima is structured around three

financing windows:

Window 2 aims to co-finance private sector

climate adaptation with public co-benefits in line

with LAP priorities – such as climate-resilient

business infrastructure and agricultural practices.

It targets for-profit actors (e.g. SMEs and

cooperatives) and provides matching grants that

complement the implementer’s financing of

project costs.

Window 3 “Private finance mobilisation”

Window 3 — which will be developed in parallel

to the implementation of Windows 1 and 2 and

operationalised at a later stage — targets

financial institutions (FI), including banks and

microfinance providers. It is intended to use

blended financing, in which public funds provide

de-risking for FI’s loan portfolios to mobilise

private capital for private sector adaptation

measures.

V. Pilot phase

The Fundo ProClima will be piloted over a period of

18 months from January 2026 to June 2027 in the

four pilot districts of Buzi (Sofala), Guijá (Gaza),

Murrupula and Ilha de Moçambique (both Nampula).

The pilot prioritises the financing of ecosystem-

based and gender-responsive LAP-measures. The

initial financing amounts to around EUR 1,000,000.
